Welcome to CAPS

We train future policy makers in both the technical and ethical dimensions of machine learning.

In an immersive six-session seminar, we train participants for the (fictional) role of forward deployed policy engineer, tasked with overseeing the development and testing of real-world AI implementation.

AI Bootcamp

Foundational training in reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms and human-AI interaction.

Reward function formalization. Agent-environment interaction. Markov decision processes. The Bellman equation. Discounting.

Tactical AI Deployment

Rule-based decision making given “fuzzy” information.

Case: Desigining and evaluating a rule-based AI system to clear a conflict zone for sake of minimizing human risk

AI System Architecture

Decision-making with and without a model of the world.

Case: Determining and evaluating likely learning architectures of an enemy drone based on observed behaviors

Human Control in Reinforcement Learning

Guiding reinforcement learning processes with human inputs (hybrid systems).

Case: Optimizing operation of a semi-autonomous convoy in contested territory following unanticipated live test results.

AI-Human Interaction

Aligning autonomous performance and human ecosystems.

Special session: Making sound predictions given partial and imperfect information; transitioning from model to real systems.

Debriefing

Participant project presentations and evaluation by a former senior policy maker engaged in emerging governance issues.
